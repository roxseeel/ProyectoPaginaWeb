Basic Transcript in English – Metabolic Diseases

This is the basic English transcript of the video about metabolic diseases, including all relevant auditory information to ensure accessibility for all users.

Introduction

Metabolic diseases are disorders that alter how the body obtains and uses energy. These diseases can result from genetic, hormonal factors, or unhealthy habits such as poor diet or lack of exercise. They affect vital functions like digestion, fat storage, and blood sugar regulation. Understanding them is essential for prevention.

Diabetes mellitus

One of the most common metabolic diseases is diabetes mellitus. In this condition, the body cannot properly regulate blood glucose levels due to lack or improper use of insulin. If left uncontrolled, it can cause damage to the eyes, kidneys, and heart.

Obesity

Obesity occurs when there is an excessive accumulation of body fat. This happens due to an imbalance between energy intake and energy expenditure. It also increases the risk of developing diabetes, hypertension, and cardiovascular diseases.

Hypothyroidism

Hypothyroidism occurs when the thyroid gland does not produce enough hormones. This slows down metabolism, causing fatigue, weight gain, and a constant feeling of cold.

Dyslipidemias

Dyslipidemias are characterized by elevated cholesterol or triglycerides in the blood. Over time, these fats can accumulate in the arteries, obstructing blood flow and increasing the risk of heart attack.

Risk Factors

Although some metabolic diseases have a genetic origin, many are related to lifestyle. A diet high in sugar and fat, sedentary habits, stress, and lack of sleep increase risk. Adopting healthy habits can make a significant difference in prevention.

Prevention

The best way to prevent metabolic diseases is to maintain a balanced diet rich in vegetables, fruits, and healthy proteins. Regular physical activity, good sleep, and periodic medical check-ups are also important. Small changes can have a big impact on your health.

Conclusion

Metabolic diseases can be prevented. Taking care of your body with healthy habits is key to maintaining a healthy metabolism and a longer, healthier life. Remember: taking care of your metabolism is taking care of your health.
